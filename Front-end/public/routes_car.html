Discover three routes that will guide you to find the gates to the history, culture, and religion of this county, amidst the vastness of nature and picturesque landscapes that connect the entire area.

The north of the county - the first route

The north of the county - the first route

The first route will remind you of personalities who have brought honor to the country, will encourage you towards spirituality, and acquaint you with the life and culture of the period from which Vaslui draws its roots.

Fâstăci Monastery

The first location on this route is Fâstăci Monastery, an architectural and spiritual gem built in Byzantine style, with a history believed to date back to the 15th century when it was constructed by a nobleman as a gratitude for a monk who saved his life. It is located in a picturesque area near the village of the same name and is surrounded by a beautiful natural landscape, abundant with forests and fields.

The Stephen the Great County Museum

The Stephen the Great County Museum encompasses seven sections from five domains, namely history, archaeology, ethnography, culture, and art. Within this museum, visitors can also observe a diorama of the Battle of Vaslui, which stands out from other battles between Christians and Turks due to the magnitude of the deployed forces, the weaponry used, the applied combat tactics, and most importantly, the ingenuity of Voivode Stephen the Great's army. Despite facing an opposing force approximately twice the size of his own, Stephen the Great, the ruler of Moldavia, managed to achieve victory.

You will continue the route with two more historical landmarks, and to reach them, you need to follow the DN24 Vaslui - Iasi road until you reach the commune of Solesti, where you will be able to visit:

The house and tomb of Elena Cuza

The next locations are situated next to each other in the village of Solesti, and they are Casa Elena Cuza or Conacul Rosetti-Solescu, which dates back to the 19th century and represents the place where the future First Lady of Romania spent her childhood, and her tomb, as it was her wish to be laid to rest in the churchyard next to the house where she grew up.

Ethnographic Museum
Satu Nou

Just 5 minutes away from the previous attractions, you will find the second museum on the route, specifically The Ethnographic Museum Satu Nou. Its role is to preserve traditional clothing items from the Romanian village, documents reflecting the spiritual life of the past, a collection of musical folklore, as well as tools used by people in rural areas for their livelihood. Visiting this museum requires making an appointment through the local council at the following number: 0784256866 .

Continuing on the DN24 Vaslui - Iasi road, after approximately 15 minutes, on the left side of the road, you can observe an arched stone gate that indicates the entrance to:

Burcel's Mound (Movila lui Burcel) and the Sfinții Împărați Constantin și Elena Monastery

The story goes that in 1498, Voivode Stephen the Great was passing through the southern area of the Moldavian Plateau on a Sunday when he was greatly surprised to see a peasant plowing the land with only one hand. When asked why he was working on Sunday, the peasant named Burcel replied that he was very poor and had no means to support his family. When asked why he was missing a hand, the peasant explained that he lost it in the Battle of Vaslui. Impressed by his story, the generous voivode gifted the peasant six bags of gold coins and granted him a document declaring that the land would forever belong to the Burcel family.
Today, on the land of the legendary Burcel's Mound, there is a beautiful church named Sfinții Împărați Constantin și Elena (Saints Emperor Constantine and Helena), as well as the renowned sculptures. One notable monument is a majestic structure composed of three crosses, believed to hold various meanings such as the Holy Trinity, the three Romanian provinces, or the three great saints of Orthodoxy. Another remarkable monument is the one depicting the Map of Greater Romania, while yet another significant sculpture is the Bronze Woman, symbolizing Trajan's Dacia.

To conclude the route, you will need to venture onto an unpaved road, passing through the villages of the county, composed of DJ426 and DJ427. Along this route, you will be surrounded by natural landscapes that will captivate your senses.

The Emil Racoviță Memorial House

After a journey through several villages in Vaslui County, we conclude the route in the commune of Danesti , where you will find the house where the renowned scientist Emil Racoviță was born and lived. This house has been transformed into a memorial museum that exhibits objects related to the scientist's childhood, photocopies, facsimiles, and memorial collections depicting his life and academic achievements.

The southwest of the county - the second route

The southwest of the county - the second route

The second route takes you through the southwestern part of the Vaslui county, starting from the city of Vaslui and crossing the western border of the county until it reaches the city of Bârlad.

Florești Monastery

Florești Monastery is known for its impressive neogothic architecture, which sets it apart from other Orthodox monasteries. It is said to be one of the oldest places of worship in Vaslui County and has been restored to preserve its beauty despite the passage of time. Near the village that bears the monastery's name, you will also find Gădeasa Lake, a perfect place to reconnect with nature, as it is situated in a picturesque setting.

The Nașterea Sfântului Ioan Botezătorul Monastery - Ivănești

The monastery was founded in the year 2000 at the request of the faithful from the Ivănești commune. It is a wooden construction with a stone foundation, in the shape of a cross. Since the monastery is dedicated to the Nativity of St. John the Baptist, the dome of the narthex is painted with St. John the Baptist and several scenes from his life. In the monastery courtyard, you can also see a bust of the ruler Stephen the Great, placed halfway between the church and the summer altar.

Despite the fact that the road from the Nașterea Sfântului Ioan Botezătorul Monastery to the next destination is more extensive, it passes through various villages that keep alive the customs and traditions of the region.

Mihai Eminescu Center - The Red House

The "Centrul Mihai Eminescu" in Bârlad is a unique concept at the national level in terms of heritage and presentation. It is the place where the masterpiece of Eminescu's work is located, and it has a collection that consists of over 5,800 volumes, out of which 2,100 are related to Mihai Eminescu. The chest in which the poet kept his manuscripts, numerous records, audio tapes, and CDs with romances based on Mihai Eminescu's verses, as well as artworks created to illustrate his literary works, can be admired during a visit to the center.

Leaving the city of Bârlad and heading back towards Vaslui, at the end of this route, you will pass by several historical monuments that cannot be missed.

The Lady's Bridge

The Chițcani Bridge was built in 1841 by Mihail Sturza with the purpose of developing the road network that connected the capital of Moldova, Iași, with the main commercial centers. The Lady's Bridge takes its name from an old postal station on the "Drumul Olacului" (Olac Road) from the 17th and 18th centuries. It is located on DN24, approximately 70 meters from the main road.

Moreni Monastery and Țiclăuani Hermitage

The impressive Moreni Monastery , located at the foot of Țiclău Hill, 27 km south of Vaslui city, is currently home to 26 nuns and sisters. Its roots can be traced back to the years 1540-1546 when Lupan Buznea built a simple wooden church on the same land. The place of worship acquired the name Moreni Monastery much later, in 1853 when another wooden church replaced the previous one. Țiclăuani Hermitage , which belongs to Moreni Monastery, is situated in a particularly picturesque area, offering a splendid view of the entire region.

The equestrian statue of Stephen the Great at Podul Înalt

The equestrian statue of Stephen the Great at Podul Înalt is a bronze monument sculpted by Mircea Ștefănescu and completed in 1975. It was created in honor of the Battle of Podul Înalt, where ruler Stephen the Great defeated the Ottoman army. The statue stands as a tribute to the valor and military achievements of Stephen the Great and serves as a reminder of the historical significance of the battle.

Vaslui County - the third route

Vaslui County - the third route

The last route, which passes through the three main cities of the county, Vaslui, Bârlad, and Huși, connects the representative landmarks of each. The route begins in the city of Vaslui, although it can be started from any of the three cities, as it is designed as a closed circuit.

The Ensemble of the Princely Courts

The route starts at a historic site that encompasses both an architectural reconstruction of the former princely court, built between 1435-1436, and the Church of Taierea Capului Sfantului Ioan Botezatorul (Beheading of St. John the Baptist), founded by Stefan cel Mare himself in 1490. It is the tourist symbol of the city of Vaslui and is visited daily by dozens of people. Moreover, the entire area represents a space conducive to relaxation in the harmony of nature, history, and spirituality.

Later on, you will head towards Bârlad, on DN24 street, along which you will be able to observe, on the left side, both the Equestrian Statue of Ștefan cel Mare at Podul Înalt and a road that will guide you to the Lady's Bridge.

Bârlad Zoo

Although initially consisting of only one bear confined in a cage, the Bârlad Zoo, established in 1959, now houses over 540 specimens from 65 species, making it the largest zoo of its kind in Moldova.

In Bârlad, you can also observe the monument erected in memory of the Bârlad-born painter Nicolae Tonitza. The monument is located at the site where his birthplace and childhood home once stood.

The Vasile Pârvan Museum

The Vasile Pârvan Museumis a museum of history and art, founded in 1947 and named in honor of the Romanian archaeologist Vasile Pârvan , who was born in Bârlad. The museum houses diverse collections that present the history and culture of the city and the surrounding region. Within the museum, you can visit six different sections: archaeology, art, history, astronomy, cultural Bârlad, and natural sciences. Its exhibits include archaeological objects, historical documents, ethnographic artifacts, paintings, and sculptures. The astronomy section features an observatory and the only digital planetarium in Vaslui County, which is also one of the few digital planetariums in the country. These two attractions can be visited within the Marcel Guguianu Exhibition Pavilion , along with a series of works by the local artist from Bârlad.

After leaving Bârlad on DN24A street and heading towards the eastern part of the county, you will make a stop at Bujoreni Monastery, which you can reach by turning left after the village of Popeni. On the way to the monastery, there is a centuries-old oak tree that is worth seeing.

Bujoreni Monastery

Bujoreni Monastery, also known as Schitul Magarului (Donkey's Hermitage), derives its name from a legend that tells the story of a shepherd who found his donkey kneeling in front of an oak tree that housed an icon of the Virgin Mary in its hollow. Inspired by this miraculous event, a hermitage was established on that spot, and the trunk of the oak tree became a holy table. Today, the monastery stands as a beautiful place of worship, open to all Christians.

The historical monument of the Battle of Țigănca

The monument erected in memory of one of the bloodiest battles fought by Romanians for the unification of the country, also known as the Battle of Țigănca , where 10,000 Romanian soldiers lost their lives or went missing, can be seen in the first historical park of Vaslui County, located in the commune of Fălciu.

The Averesti Winery

Once you reach Husi , the city among the vineyards, you will encounter the renowned Averesti Wine Center. Here, the vine has been cultivated for thousands of years, with the trade being passed down from father to son. Each year, in the coolest corner of the cellar, a bottle of the first wine produced is buried as an offering to the vineyards and those who have worked on those lands. Certainly, the Averesti Estates are worth a visit and, why not, leaving behind after acquiring a bottle of wine chosen according to personal preference from the multitude of types available.

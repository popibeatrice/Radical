A selection of routes highlighting essential tourist attractions, historical sites, and cultural landmarks of the county, both from the county seat and the city of Bârlad.

The city of Vaslui - the first route

The city of Vaslui - the first route

A route that guides you from south to north through the rich gates of the county's history, along paths embraced by natural beauty, and up to the vibrant living treasure of its heritage, where the past comes to life and welcomes you into a world full of stories and fascinating artifacts.

The Ensemble of the Princely Courts Princely Courts

The first route includes a historic site that encompasses both an architectural reconstruction of the former princely court, built between 1435-1436, and the Church of Taierea Capului Sfantului Ioan Botezatorul (Beheading of St. John the Baptist), founded by Stefan cel Mare himself in 1490. It is the tourist symbol of the city of Vaslui and is visited daily by dozens of people. Moreover, the entire area represents a space conducive to relaxation in the harmony of nature, history, and spirituality.

On the way to the Summer Theater, less than five minutes from the Princely Court, on the right side of the road, you will be able to observe Mavrocordat House, a historic building constructed between 1883 and 1892. Over time, it has been owned by various noble families, but now it hosts various courses in which children can participate.

Summer Theater

An imposing structure within the city center, located right in the heart of the park that bears its name, the Summer Theater is the host of performances and film evenings held in the city. The Summer Theater Park, also known as Youth Park (Parcul Tineretului), is the place in the city where the first natural gas flame was ignited. A significant part of this park consists of a vast green area, adorned with benches inviting visitors to take a break from the daily stress. It is also home to an artistic fountain an artistic fountain, a work weighing over 15 tons, created by architect Nicolae Munteanu in 1975, at the same time as the park's construction.

On the other side of the city center, you will find Civic Square (Piața Civică), in the middle of which stands the statue of the ruler Stefan cel Mare, erected in 1975 by Iftimie Barleanu.

The Stephen the Great County Museum

The Stephen the Great County Museum encompasses seven sections from five domains, namely history, archaeology, ethnography, culture, and art. Within this museum, visitors can also observe a diorama of the Battle of Vaslui, which stands out from other battles between Christians and Turks due to the magnitude of the deployed forces, the weaponry used, the applied combat tactics, and most importantly, the ingenuity of Voivode Stephen the Great's army. Despite facing an opposing force approximately twice the size of his own, Stephen the Great, the ruler of Moldavia, managed to achieve victory.

Independence Square Park

Behind Civic Square, you will find Piața Independenței , also known as Secu Park due to its proximity to the former headquarters of the Securitate (the Communist-era secret police). The area is populated throughout the day, with older individuals gathering in the morning to play chess or backgammon. In the evenings, the park is filled with teenagers eager to socialize. In Secu Park, you can also find the largest chessboard in Romania, with human-sized chess pieces set up on special occasions. Additionally, there is a monument erected in honor of the fallen heroes who fought for the freedom and independence of the country.

Before reaching the final stop of the route, as you climb towards Copou Park, you will pass by the Monument of the Fallen Heroes in the Second World War.

Copou Park

Copou Park, known as one of the most beautiful parks in Moldova, harmoniously combines natural elements and landscaped designs to create a perfect space for recreation and relaxation. The tall and dense trees spanning over the 10 hectares of the parkprovide a refuge during hot summers. Moreover, within the park, there is a dedicated playground for children and the Writers' Alley (Aleea Scriitorilor).

The city of Bârlad - the second route

The city of Bârlad - the second route

In contrast, the route from Bârlad starts from the northern part of the city and extends to the southeastern part, reaching both the distant roots of important personalities of the country and the border between land and space.

Bârlad Zoo

Although initially consisting of only one bear confined in a cage, the Bârlad Zoo, established in 1959, now houses over 540 specimens from 65 species, making it the largest zoo of its kind in Moldova.

Public Garden

It is one of the oldest and most beautiful public gardens of its kind in Moldova and carries a legend that is worth knowing. It is said that Prince Mihail Sturdza, who had encountered a "beautiful lady" in Bârlad, stopped here on his way back from Constantinople in 1834 and ordered the authorities of the Tutova Land to bring saplings of the finest trees to "create a garden" as a tribute to the beautiful ladies of Bârlad.

In Bârlad, you can also observe the monument erected in memory of the Bârlad-born painter Nicolae Tonitza. The monument is located at the site where his birthplace and childhood home once stood.

Mihai Eminescu Center - The Red House

The "Centrul Mihai Eminescu"is a unique concept at the national level in terms of heritage and presentation. It is the place where the masterpiece of Eminescu's work is located, and it has a collection that consists of over 5,800 volumes, out of which 2,100 are related to Mihai Eminescu. The chest in which the poet kept his manuscripts, numerous records, audio tapes, and CDs with romances based on Mihai Eminescu's verses, as well as artworks created to illustrate his literary works, can be admired during a visit to the center.

The Vasile Pârvan Museum

The Vasile Pârvan Museumis a museum of history and art, founded in 1947 and named in honor of the Romanian archaeologist Vasile Pârvan , who was born in Bârlad. The museum houses diverse collections that present the history and culture of the city and the surrounding region. Within the museum, you can visit six different sections: archaeology, art, history, astronomy, cultural Bârlad, and natural sciences. Its exhibits include archaeological objects, historical documents, ethnographic artifacts, paintings, and sculptures. The astronomy section features an observatory and the only digital planetarium in Vaslui County, which is also one of the few digital planetariums in the country. These two attractions can be visited within the Marcel Guguianu Exhibition Pavilion , along with a series of works by the local artist from Bârlad.

The Sturza House - The Collections Museum

The Sturza House was built in 1812 by the noble family Ștefan Sturdza Bârlădeanu and his wife Safta. It is now part of the sections of the Vasile Pârvan Museum, specifically known as the Collections Museum, where it houses numerous art collections. The Sturza House is a significant architectural and historical landmark, showcasing the grandeur and elegance of the period in which it was constructed. Within the museum, visitors can explore a variety of art collections, offering insights into different artistic styles, periods, and mediums. It provides a captivating journey through the world of art and serves as a testament to the cultural heritage of the region.

Victor Ion Popa Theater

Inaugurated since 1955, the Victor Ion Popa Theater has been the host of numerous theater productions based on texts from Romanian and international dramatic literature from its inception until today.

Alexandru Ioan Cuza Memorial House

In the Podeni neighborhood, on Alexandru Ioan Cuza Street, No. 95AS, is the house where the ruler of the United Principalities, Alexandru Ioan Cuza, was born.
